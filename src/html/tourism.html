Longido is one of the six districts in the Arusha region of Tanzania, spanning an area of 7,885 kmÂ² with a population of approximately 180,000. Located in the northeast, it boasts undeveloped agroecological resources, stunning landscapes, and abundant wildlife.

Where to Visit?

Cultural Tourism: Meet the Maasai

The Maasai are the predominant group in Longido, leading a pastoralist lifestyle. Women build mud-and-grass bomas, while men and children tend to livestock. Warriors (morani) and spiritual leaders (loiboni) ensure community security.

Mountain Adventures

Explore renowned peaks like Oldonyo Lengai, Mount Meru, Longido-Namanga, Gelai, and Ketumbeini. These mountains offer breathtaking vistas and diverse landscapes.

Lake Natron: Nature's Tranquility

Lake Natron is home to millions of flamingos and offers unique opportunities for nature enthusiasts. Enjoy photographic tourism and witness the beauty of this natural wonder.

Rare and Colorful Birds

Encounter rare and colorful birds such as red-and-yellow barbets (Trachyphonus erythrocephalus), silverbills (Empidornis semipartitus), and secretary birds. The plains and slopes also host diverse wildlife, including giraffes, zebras, gazelles, elephants, lions, and more.

Additional Attractions

Longido offers various activities, including hunting blocks, campsites around Lake Natron, viewing active volcano eruptions at Oldonyo Lengai, and exploring game-controlled areas with abundant fauna.
